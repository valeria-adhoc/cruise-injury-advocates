Privacy Policy

How Cruise Injury Advocates handles the information you share through this website.

Cruise Injury Advocates, the cruise-injury practice of Suro & Rodriguez, PLLC ("we," "us," or "our"), respects your privacy. This policy explains what information we collect through this website, how we use it, and the choices you have.

Information we collect

When you submit a contact form on this site, we collect the information you choose to provide: your name, email address, phone number, and the details of your message. We also receive standard technical information that your browser sends automatically, such as your IP address, device and browser type, and the pages you view, through our website host and any analytics we use.

How we use your information

We use the information you provide to respond to your inquiry, evaluate a potential matter, and communicate with you about it. We use technical information to operate, secure, and improve the website. We do not sell your personal information.

How your information is processed and shared

Contact-form submissions are transmitted to and processed through our client-management provider so that we can follow up with you. This website is hosted on Cloudflare Pages and uses Google Fonts and an embedded Google Map; those providers may receive technical data as a normal part of delivering those features. We may share information with service providers who help us operate the site and our practice, or when required by law or to protect our rights. We do not otherwise disclose your personal information to third parties for their own marketing.

Cookies and similar technologies

This site uses only the cookies and local storage needed for its basic operation and for features such as the embedded map. If we add analytics or advertising technologies in the future, we will update this policy accordingly.

Data retention

We keep the information you submit only as long as necessary to respond to your inquiry and to meet our legal, professional, and record-keeping obligations, after which we take reasonable steps to delete or de-identify it.

Your choices and rights

You may contact us to ask what information we hold about you, or to request that we correct or delete it, subject to our legal and professional obligations. Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under applicable privacy laws; contact us and we will respond consistent with those laws.

Important notice

Submitting a form or contacting us through this website does not create an attorney-client relationship, and information you send is not protected as confidential until an attorney-client relationship is established in writing. Please do not send sensitive, confidential, or time-sensitive information through the contact form.

Contact us

If you have questions about this policy or how we handle your information, call us at (786) 814-6427, or visit our office at The Wells Fargo Center, 333 SE 2nd Ave., Suite 2000, Miami, FL 33131.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version is always posted on this page.

This page is general information, not legal advice.
