Services

We represent passengers and crew injured aboard ship and ashore. If your situation isn't listed, ask, we likely handle it.

For Passengers

When the trip goes wrong.

If you were hurt as a guest on a cruise, these are the cases we handle most.

Slip, Trip & Fall Onboard

Wet decks, pool areas, stairs, and poorly maintained walkways that cause serious falls.

Learn more

Shore Excursion Injuries

Injuries on cruise-sold excursions: snorkeling, zip-lines, watercraft, and tours.

Learn more

Medical Negligence at Sea

Delayed, missed, or substandard care from the ship's infirmary when it mattered most.

Learn more

Assault & Onboard Crime

Inadequate security, over-service of alcohol, and crew or passenger misconduct.

Learn more

Overboard & Drowning

Man-overboard incidents, pool and water-slide drownings, and failures to respond or rescue.

Learn more

Wrongful Death at Sea

Compassionate, determined representation for families who lost a loved one on a cruise.

Learn more
For Crew & Seafarers

Protecting the people who work at sea.

Crew members have distinct rights under maritime law. We help seafarers injured aboard the vessel.

Jones Act Claims

Compensation for seamen injured by an employer's or co-worker's negligence aboard a vessel.

Learn more

Maintenance & Cure

Daily living and medical-care benefits owed to crew who fall ill or are injured in service.

Learn more

Unseaworthiness

Claims when a vessel, its equipment, or its crew is not reasonably fit for its purpose.

Learn more

Death on the High Seas Act

Federal remedies for families when a death occurs beyond U.S. territorial waters.

Learn more

We represent both passengers and crew, including seafarers pursuing Jones Act, maintenance and cure, unseaworthiness, and Death on the High Seas Act claims.
